T-Mobile

I was a UX Designer on a team based in Redmond, WA, working on a business-to-business communication platform, based on T-Mobile’s publicly available DIGITs application. I worked on two applications of the project, designing for mobile, tablet, and desktop.

The first application is an admin portal that allows for management of users and accounts, devices, phone lines, and IVR trees (the automated “press 0 to speak with an operator” menu systems). The second was the communication application, which allows for the end user to manage multiple phone lines on a single device. A majority of my work was on the settings, calling, and voicemail features.

My responsibilities were mostly confined to the early stages of product design, primarily developing the information architecture and generating user flows, creating and testing with wireframes and prototypes, and early (rough) visual design. I also was responsible for ensuring this work met the changing engineering capabilities as well as the evolving internal and external customer priorities.

A majority of this project isn’t public facing, and even if it were, it’s a B2B app, so I’d never see it unless I worked for the as of yet unannounced customer(s).
