30 YEARS OF SATELLITE OBSERVATION

In late 1979, the US began continous, space based observations of Earth, recording conditions across the globe. This allows us to build an accurate, 30+ year, month by month and day-to-daymodel of sea ice conditions in the northern hemisphere. Included here is a snapshot of conditions at the winter and summer peaks, March and September respectively, where ice coverage is at its lowest and highest.
