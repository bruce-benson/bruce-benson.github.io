T‑MOBILE DIGITS

UX DESIGN

Client: T-Mobile Agency: Collabera

Digits is a native mobile and desktop web app that allows T-Mobile customers to add multiple (T-Mobile) phone lines to a single device. This allows customers to bring their own device to the workplace, where they could add and travel with their business lines, whether traditional PTSN telephony or VOIP line, such as calling with a number or an email address. When I joined the Digits team, our goal was maintain the B2C Digits app in-flight, and to repackage the Digits app as a B2B platform for enterprise and government customers.

There were two main tasks associated with this work. The first task was a visual and UI rebranding treatment to reflect the customers’ brand identity system, while maintaining accessibility. The second task was to move from a customer managed data administration to a centrally controlled system. The purpose behind this was to allow organizations to manage contacts and data record retention in accordance with national and local laws.

During my time at T-Mobile I was on a two-person team responsible for the following features: audio/video calling, voicemail, visual voicemail (transcription), settings, and contact management. Our work on the B2B app would generally start with a competitive analysis to identify practices and patterns, solution review by senior designers, wireframe review with engineers, and hand-off to visual designers for brand specific modifications.

The screenshots on this page show my work on updates to the B2C app, as the B2B specific work is protected under NDA and I was unable to retain any of this work.
