Xbox Game Pass &
Azure Playfab

I spent six months as a Designer in the Xbox Design Studios on both Xbox Game Pass, a Netflix-like service for video games, and Azure Playfab, a full stack LiveOps tool for video games (managing services, LiveOps, and analytics). I spent a majority of my time working to building design libraries for these services, using both Sketch and Invision’s DSM (Design System Manager).

My role for each team was to create a centralized design library, where design teams could access responsive Sketch components, and developers had access to pixel perfect design wireframes. This meant, for Playfab especially, the auditing, consolidating, and updating of UI patterns and components, ensuring everything from the type ramp to accessibility requirements were met by design. If you ever want to spend an hour or two talking about design systems, grids and templates and all, just give me a call.

Neither the work I did for the Xbox Game Pass app or the Azure Playfab is public facing yet, so I’m not going to show much. I will say for Playfab, there’s lots of responsive tables with a variety of type treatments and all the rows and columns you can imagine - so it’s not that inviting a visual lol.
